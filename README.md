Preceding questioning Prompting:
### Climate Change and Greenhouse Gases

Climate change is a global concern because it affects weather patterns, sea levels, agriculture, water resources, ecosystems, and human health across the world. Rising global temperatures can lead to extreme events such as floods, droughts, heat waves, and stronger storms. It also threatens biodiversity and can impact food production and economies worldwide.

Greenhouse gases contribute to global warming by trapping heat in the Earth’s atmosphere. Gases such as carbon dioxide, methane, and nitrous oxide form a layer around the planet that prevents heat from escaping into space. This process is called the greenhouse effect. Human activities like burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes increase the amount of these gases, causing the Earth’s temperature to rise over time.

---

### How Vaccines Work and the Process of Immunization

Vaccines help protect the body from harmful diseases by training the immune system to recognize and fight germs such as viruses or bacteria. A vaccine usually contains a weakened, inactive, or harmless part of a microorganism.

When a person receives a vaccine, the immune system responds by producing antibodies. These antibodies remember how to fight the disease. Later, if the actual germ enters the body, the immune system quickly recognizes it and destroys it before the person becomes seriously ill.

This process is called immunization. It helps individuals stay healthy and also protects communities by reducing the spread of infectious diseases.
